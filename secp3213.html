Power BI Lab

Format: Interactive Dashboard Design & DAX Implementation

VanArsdel Sales Overview Dashboard

Project Overview: Developed a comprehensive, interactive Power BI dashboard to analyze VanArsdel's historical sales and supply chain data (2000–2016). The objective was to transform raw multi-regional sales records into an executive-level summary focusing on revenue trends, market segments, and Year-to-Date (YTD) performance.

  • Data Modeling & DAX: Implemented complex DAX measures to calculate critical business metrics, including YTD Revenue, Last Year (LY) YTD Revenue, and YTD Sales Variance across different manufacturers.
  • Visual Analytics: Designed a multi-faceted report utilizing Waterfall charts for manufacturer variance, Area charts for category trends over time, and geographical Maps to pinpoint unit distribution across North America, Europe, and Australia.
  • Automated Insights: Integrated Smart Narrative visuals and Gauge KPIs to instantly generate readable text summaries of highest-performing regions and total gross revenue (RM 1.03B).

Reflection: Building this dashboard reinforced the importance of choosing the right visual for the right metric. For instance, using a Waterfall chart made it instantly clear which manufacturers were driving negative sales variance. Furthermore, writing DAX for time-intelligence functions (like YTD vs LY YTD) proved how vital underlying data models are to dynamic reporting.

Business Intelligence Project

Format: Group Dashboard Design & Implementation

End-to-End BI Workflow and Interactive Dashboard

Project Overview: Developed a complete Business Intelligence solution to analyze retail sales performance and customer purchasing behavior. The project integrated three distinct datasets—pricing signals, product catalogs, and historical sales transactions—to evaluate product demand, pricing behavior, and sales trends. By utilizing Alteryx Designer for data preparation and Power BI for interactive visualization, the objective was to transform raw retail data into actionable business intelligence for strategic decision-making.

  • Phase 1 (Data Prep & Modeling): The data preparation pipeline was engineered entirely in Alteryx Designer. The process involved data cleaning steps like replacing nulls in the promotion type field and filtering out zero-sales events. The datasets were then integrated using two sequential left joins—first at the product level, then at the category level—and aggregated to a category-month grain before exporting as a unified CSV.
  • Phase 2 (Dashboard & Storytelling): Two interactive dashboards and one storyboard were created using Microsoft Power BI Desktop. Visualizations included KPI Cards for top-level metrics, Clustered Bar Charts for product tier comparisons, and a Region x Category Heatmap. The dashboards incorporated interactive slicers for Region, Category, and Product Tier to enable on-demand, cross-dimensional exploration.
  • Outcome: The analysis revealed that Clothing & Apparel generated more than double the revenue of any other category. It also highlighted that deeper discount rates on Mid-Range products (~20%) did not result in a significant demand response compared to Premium products (~14%). Additionally, Winter was identified as the critical sales season, contributing 67.18% of the overall revenue.

Reflection: A major technical challenge during the workflow development was managing data quality issues, specifically product ID data type mismatches and category naming inconsistencies across the independently sourced datasets. However, enforcing a reproducible ETL process in Alteryx minimized manual handling and guaranteed that the data fed into Power BI was consistent, correctly typed, and fully analysis-ready.
